# Gematria Systems — Complete Reference

Gematria is the practice of assigning numerical values to letters, revealing numerical relationships within words, phrases, and texts. The word derives from the Greek *geometria* (geometry) through Aramaic transliteration. Its use in Jewish tradition is attested from at least the Second Temple period (c. 200 BCE), with roots possibly in Babylonian and Hellenistic influence.

> *"The letters of the Torah are fire, black fire on white fire."* — Zohar, Bereshit

### 2. Mispar Siduri (מספר סידורי) — Hebrew Ordinal

Each letter = its position in the alphabet (1–22). The 22 letters of the Hebrew alphabet map to ordinal numbers 1 through 22, compressing the value space.

**Key property:** Maximum single letter = 22 (Tav). Maximum word value = 22 × word_length.

**Extraordinary coincidence:** YHWH in ordinal = 10+5+6+5 = **26** — same as in standard. This unique numerical agreement is noted extensively in gematria literature.

Letter ordinal values (positions 1-22): א=1, ב=2, ג=3, ד=4, ה=5, ו=6, ז=7, ח=8, ט=9, י=10, כ=11, ל=12, מ=13, נ=14, ס=15, ע=16, פ=17, צ=18, ק=19, ר=20, ש=21, ת=22.

### 3. Mispar Katan (מספר קטן) — Hebrew Reduced

Digital root reduction: every letter value collapses to a single digit 1-9.

**Reduction formula:** katan(v) = ((v−1) mod 9) + 1

The three tiers of the alphabet (units 1-9, tens 10-90, hundreds 100-900) all reduce to the *same* nine digits. The 22-letter alphabet cycles: 1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9,1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9,1,2,3,4.

**The 9-cycle:** Nine represents completion in Kabbalistic numerology. The reduction reveals the 9-fold structure underlying all 22 letters. EMET (truth) = 1+40+400 → 1+4+4 = 9 = completion.

### 8. English Reverse — Z=1 through A=26

Mirror of ordinal: Z=1, Y=2, ..., A=26. The fundamental property: **ordinal(L) + reverse(L) = 27** for any letter L. The mirror constant 27 = 3³.

**Fixed point:** LOVE = 54 in both ordinal AND reverse — a **palindromic fixed point**. LOVE is invariant under reflection.
